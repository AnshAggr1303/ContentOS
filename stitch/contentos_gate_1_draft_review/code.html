STORY SLUG FIN-INDIA-RIL-Q3

Reliance Industries maintains aggressive growth trajectory as retail and digital segments offset global energy volatility.

MUMBAI — Reliance Industries Ltd (RIL) on Friday reported a consolidated net profit of ₹19,641 crore for the October-December quarter, a performance that analysts are calling "exceptionally resilient" given the macro-economic headwinds facing the global energy sector. The results underscore the conglomerate's successful pivot toward a consumer-centric ecosystem.

Gross revenue for the quarter stood at ₹2.48 lakh crore, up 3.2% year-on-year. The star performers were the company's retail and digital services divisions, which now contribute nearly half of the group's overall EBITDA. Reliance Retail, in particular, saw a massive footfall of 282 million customers during the festive season, cementing its position as India's undisputed retail leader.

Meanwhile, the Oil-to-Chemicals (O2C) segment faced challenges from lower refining margins globally. However, the company's superior configuration and ability to optimize feedstock helped maintain operational stability. Analysts expect the next phase of growth to be driven by the rollout of the New Energy business, which is slated for commercialization in late 2025.
